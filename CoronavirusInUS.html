Coronavirus (COVID-19) in the U.S.

Tooltips and filters are available. Use your mouse to hover over any bars on the bar chart to see its tooltip, and drill-down combobox to filter the information by Month.

The coronavirus (COVID-19) disease continues to spread around the world, with around 14.66 million cases and 609,100 deaths as of July 20, 2020. COVID-19 is thought to spread mainly through close contact from person-to-person. Some people without symptoms may be able to spread the virus. In the United States, the number of infections has risen dramatically since the first week of March, and the U.S. now has more confirmed cases and deaths than any other country worldwide. All 50 states have been affected, but New York has the highest number of deaths and has reported more cases than any single country outside of the United States.
