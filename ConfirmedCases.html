State-by-state: Trends in coronavirus cases

Tooltips and filters are available. Use your mouse to hover over any bars on the bar chart to see its tooltip, and drill-down combobox to filter the information by state.

This chart provides the number of confirmed cases of novel coronavirus over the past few months by state. More than 3,784,920 people in the United States have been infected with the coronavirus. Case numbers are surging throughout most of the United States, including in many states that were among the first to reopen. Because the number of people hospitalized and the percentage of people testing positive is also rising in many of those places, the case spike cannot be solely explained by increased testing.

To put that in perspective, the first reported case came on January 21. After 99 days, 1 million Americans became infected.It took just 43 days after that to reach 2 million cases. And 28 days later, on July 8, the US reached 3 million cases. The 4 millionth case could come just two weeks after that. More governors are requiring masks, and dozens of hospitals are out of intensive care unit beds. The current surge in coronavirus cases in the United States is being driven by states that were among the first to reopen their economies, decisions that epidemiologists warned could lead to a wave of infections.
