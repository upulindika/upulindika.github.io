Coronavirus Pandemic (COVID-19)

Tooltips are available. Use the mouse to hover over any dots, maps on the chart to see its tooltip.

Authorities in 214 countries and territories have reported about 15.1 million Covid‑19 cases and 620,000 deaths since China reported its first cases to the World Health Organization (WHO) in December. Only if we end the pandemic everywhere can we end the pandemic anywhere. The entire world has the same goal: cases of COVID-19 need to go to zero. The scatter plot below shows which each countries number of confirmed cases vs their population. To be safe anywhere, every region in the world needs to make progress against the pandemic – and this means colored dots disappearing from the plot. Progress is possible – some countries bent the curve of new cases and are monitoring the outbreak well. But globally we are very far from the goal and the global number of confirmed cases is rising extremely fast.

Since January, novel coronavirus has spread to nearly every state and territory.

The number of COVID-19 cases worldwide has surpassed 15 million, according to data from Johns Hopkins University. The United States still has the highest numbers, with nearly 4 million cases as of Jul 16, 2020, followed by Brazil with 2.1 million cases, and India with 1.1 million cases. The worldwide death toll from COVID-19 is over 618,000, according to Johns Hopkins.

As of now the US is on track to hit 1 million new cases in 2 weeks. The presentation has attempted to track and do analysis on COVID-19 cases of US over next three scenes.
